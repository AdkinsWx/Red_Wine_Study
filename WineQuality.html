Tasting Wine By the Numbers by Matthew Adkins

This tidy data set contains 1,599 red wines with 11 variables on the chemical properties of the wine. At least 3 wine experts rated the quality of each wine, providing a rating between 0 (very bad) and 10 (very excellent). Here we will explore which chemical properties influence the taste of red wines.

Reflection

This project data set consisted of 1599 observations and eleven attributes that could effect overall quality of the wine. Surprisingly, certain factors that should have a direct effect on taste, and therefore quality, were not always enough to determine a wine's' quality rating. Factors such as citric acid directly determine a wine's' flavor however it did not change the wine's' quality rating. Other factors such as alcohol content very strongly determined a wine's' rating. In the case of alcohol content more is better.

We were also able to see that fixed acidity correlated with density. This implies that the process that raises fixed acidity also creates a denser wine. We are unable to determine if this effects the flavor of the wine but it does counter-act the density lowering factor of the alcohol content in the wine.

A final thought is that we found that our data set consisted of rather mild red wines and did not contain any of the “sweet” varieties, wine with 45 g/l or more. Thus in our data set residual sugar was not a determining factor when analyzing our wines. In futures it would be interesting to study residual sugar in wines that are meant to be sweeter. Would more residual sugar lend to better quality wines? Or does this variable only become useful in classifying the kind of wine in the data set?
